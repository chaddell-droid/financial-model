# Server Memory Sector — Cycle, Pricing & Valuation Context
**Date: May 10, 2026**
**Coverage: Memory cycle dynamics, sector multiples, and bull/bear debate**

---

## Executive Summary

The memory cycle has entered what bulls call a "structural AI super-cycle" and bears call "the sharpest cyclical peak ever." Contract DRAM pricing is up 58–63% QoQ in 2Q26, HBM is fully sold out at all three majors through 2026 (with allocations being negotiated for 2027), DRAM industry inventory has collapsed to 2–3 weeks at SK Hynix and ~6 weeks at Samsung versus a normal 10–12 weeks, and SK Hynix posted a record 72% operating margin in 1Q26. Multiples have re-rated dramatically — Micron trades at ~15x EV/EBITDA (10-year median 6.7x, peak 69x), SK Hynix at ~6.8x P/B (5-year low 0.8x), and the picks-and-shovels (LRCX, AMAT, ASML, BESI) are pricing in a multi-year capex super-cycle. The decisive question is duration: bulls argue HBM physics (TSV, hybrid bonding, CoWoS) breaks normal cyclicality; bears point to Samsung's 50% HBM capacity ramp and SK Hynix doubling DRAM capacity in 2H26 as classic late-cycle behavior.

## 1. Where Are We in the Memory Cycle?

### 1.1 DDR5 Server DRAM Contract Pricing

The pricing trajectory through April/May 2026 is unambiguously up:

- **2Q26 outlook**: Conventional DRAM contract prices expected to rise **58–63% QoQ** in 2Q26, per TrendForce (Mar 31, 2026 release). Server DRAM is the priority allocation given superior wafer profitability ([TrendForce, Mar 31, 2026](https://www.trendforce.com/presscenter/news/20260331-12995.html)).
- **1Q26 record**: Memory contract price increases hit "record QoQ highs" across all product categories ([TrendForce, Feb 2, 2026](https://www.trendforce.com/presscenter/news/20260202-12911.html)).
- **DDR5 vs HBM3e**: TrendForce (Oct 2025) projected 2026 DDR5 server profitability would *surpass HBM3e* — a remarkable late-cycle indicator ([TrendForce, Oct 29, 2025](https://www.trendforce.com/presscenter/news/20251029-12758.html)).
- **Spot vs contract**: As of March 2026 spot prices were already trading **above** contract prices, classically a late-cycle indicator that supply is failing to meet demand ([TrendForce, Mar 25, 2026](https://www.trendforce.com/news/2026/03/25/insights-memory-spot-price-update-dram-spot-momentum-constrained-as-march-contracts-hold-ddr4-weakest/)). DDR4 spot has begun softening (China-specific speculation crackdown driving DDR4 weakness, [DigiTimes, May 4, 2026](https://www.digitimes.com/news/a20260504PD215/china-ddr4-nand-dram-demand-2026.html)).

### 1.2 Bit Growth vs Bit Demand and Inventory

Inventory has been drained to historically low levels:

- **Industry inventory**: Global DRAM inventory at **2–3 weeks** at SK Hynix and ~**6 weeks** at Samsung (Jan 2026) versus 10–12 week normal and a peak of **31 weeks in 1Q23** at the cycle trough ([TechInsights blog](https://www.techinsights.com/blog/memory-market-outlook-ai-demand-and-tight-supply-drive-resurgence)).
- **2026 supply growth**: IDC expects 2026 DRAM bit supply growth at only **~16% YoY** — below historical norms — versus demand pulled higher by HBM TSV overhead ([IDC blog, 2026](https://www.idc.com/resource-center/blog/global-memory-shortage-crisis-market-analysis-and-the-potential-impact-on-the-smartphone-and-pc-markets-in-2026/)).
- **Bit demand pivot**: HBM consumes ~23% of total DRAM wafer capacity in 2026 — a structural drain on commodity DRAM bit supply because each HBM wafer effectively "loses" ~30–50% of bits to TSV/stacking overhead ([Tech-Insider, 2026](https://tech-insider.org/memory-chip-shortage-2026-ai-consumer-electronics/)).

### 1.3 HBM Sold-Out Commentary

Allocation is fully booked through 2026 and being negotiated for 2027:

- **SK Hynix**: All DRAM, NAND, and HBM production for 2026 sold out, much of it locked to Nvidia ([NotebookCheck](https://www.notebookcheck.net/SK-hynix-sells-out-its-DRAM-NAND-and-HBM-chip-supply-to-Nvidia-through-2026-as-AI-demand-outpaces-Samsung-and-Micron-s-capacity.1151402.0.html)).
- **Micron**: AI memory product contracts for 2026 sold out, announced January 2026 ([TrendForce, Aug 2025](https://www.trendforce.com/news/2025/08/13/news-hbm-battle-heats-up-micron-reportedly-hints-2026-sell-out-sk-hynix-yet-to-confirm)).
- **Samsung**: Began HBM4 mass production for Nvidia Vera Rubin in February 2026; tripling HBM revenue YoY ([Samsung Newsroom, Q1 2026](https://news.samsung.com/global/samsung-electronics-announces-first-quarter-2026-results)).
- **Industry consensus**: HBM tightness extends into 2027, with capacity relief only after 2027 (SK Hynix Yongin) and 2028 (Samsung P5) ([Tom's Hardware](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/artificial-intelligence/samsung-and-sk-hynix-warn-ai-driven-memory-shortages-could-last-until-2027-and-beyond-as-hbm-demand-explodes-customers-already-reserving-supply-years-ahead-while-the-wider-dram-market-begins-to-tighten)).
- **2025 HBM share**: SK Hynix 62%, Micron 21%, Samsung 17% ([Astute Group](https://www.astutegroup.com/news/general/sk-hynix-holds-62-of-hbm-micron-overtakes-samsung-2026-battle-pivots-to-hbm4/)).

### 1.4 Wafer Capacity Discipline

The picture is mixed — discipline holding for HBM but commodity DRAM is now expanding:

- **Samsung**: Targets HBM at **250k wafers/month by end-2026** (+47% from 170k); also pushing 1c DRAM to **200k wafers/month** (1/3 of total output) ([TrendForce, Dec 30, 2025](https://www.trendforce.com/news/2025/12/30/news-samsung-reportedly-plans-50-hbm-capacity-surge-in-2026-spotlight-on-hbm4/)).
- **SK Hynix**: Plans to **double DRAM wafer input to ~600k/month in 2H26** to match Samsung; M15X fab ramping HBM4 ([TrendForce, Oct 2, 2025](https://www.trendforce.com/news/2025/10/02/news-sk-hynix-reportedly-to-double-dram-capacity-in-2h26-to-match-samsung-pulls-back-on-nand/)).
- **Micron**: 2026 capex projected at **$13.5B (+23% YoY)**, focused on 1-gamma node and TSV equipment ([Longyield Substack](https://longyield.substack.com/p/micron-fires-on-all-cylinders-as-ai-turns-dram-and-hbm-into-scarce-assets)).
- **Industry capex**: TrendForce describes 2026 capex as "cautious" with limited impact on bit supply growth ([TrendForce, Nov 13, 2025](https://www.trendforce.com/presscenter/news/20251113-12780.html)).

**Read**: Capacity is expanding but the additions are skewed toward HBM/1c nodes, and the meaningful new capacity (SK Hynix Yongin, Micron Japan/Idaho) doesn't translate to wafers until 2027–2028. Discipline is holding for now but the 2H26 SK Hynix DRAM doubling is the canary worth watching.

### 1.5 Spot vs Contract Spread

- Spot DRAM prices surpassed contract prices in late 2025 / early 2026 (a buying-panic indicator) before reversing modestly from a March 2026 peak ([TrendForce, Nov 12, 2025](https://www.trendforce.com/news/2025/11/12/insights-memory-spot-price-update-dram-chip-prices-surpass-module-prices-signaling-imminent-surge/), [TrendForce, Apr 15, 2026](https://www.trendforce.com/news/2026/04/15/insights-memory-spot-price-update-dram-spot-sellers-hold-firm-ahead-of-mid-april-pricing-ddr4-edges-lower-0-48/)).
- Late-cycle interpretation: When spot is above contract, distributors are panic-buying and contract negotiations are about to ratchet up. When spot then rolls over before contracts do, cycle peak is being formed.

## 2. Historical Cycle Context

### 2.1 Last Peak (2017–2018)

- 4Q18 was the previous "memory profitability above foundries" peak — a benchmark not eclipsed until 4Q25 ([Dr. Robert Castellano via Substack](https://drrobertcastellano.substack.com/p/sk-hynix-dethrones-samsung-in-dram)).
- SK Hynix annual operating margin reached ~50% in 2018; Samsung memory unit similarly elevated.
- DRAM industry revenue peaked ~$100B (2018) before collapsing ~40% in 2019.

### 2.2 Last Trough (2H22–1H23)

- Memory makers experienced one of the worst down-cycles in history; prices plunged and all three majors posted operating losses ([RAM Shortage 2025, IntuitionLabs](https://intuitionlabs.ai/articles/ram-shortage-2025-ai-demand)).
- Industry inventory peaked at **31 weeks** in 1Q23 (vs ~10–12 weeks normal).
- Micron and SK Hynix cut DRAM wafer starts by **20–30%** in early 2023 to address the glut.
- SK Hynix P/B bottomed at **0.8x in December 2022** — the trough valuation observation ([Yahoo Finance / GuruFocus](https://www.gurufocus.com/term/pettm/HXSCL)).

### 2.3 Current Cycle — What Inning?

Hard data points to a peak being formed in 2026, with key calls:

- **Operating margin**: SK Hynix posted **72% operating margin in 1Q26** ([Digitimes](https://www.digitimes.com/news/a20260423VL200/sk-hynix-profit-revenue-2026-operating-profit.html)) and **79% EBITDA margin** ([SK Hynix IR](https://news.skhynix.com/q1-2026-business-results/)). Samsung memory operating margin estimated at ~50%+ in 4Q25 ([TrendForce, Dec 23, 2025](https://www.trendforce.com/news/2025/12/23/news-memory-price-surge-reportedly-to-push-samsung-sk-hynix-gross-margins-above-tsmc-in-4q25)).
- **Comparison to 2018**: 1Q26 margins are *materially above* the 2018 peak.
- **Bull case**: HBM is structurally tight (CoWoS bottleneck at TSMC, hybrid bonding capacity at BESI, TSV equipment at AMAT/Lam) — these are physical, multi-year constraints that don't react to spot DRAM pricing. AI capex is a step-change in demand.
- **Bear case**: Samsung +50% HBM capacity, SK Hynix doubling DRAM wafer input in 2H26, Micron +23% capex — these are classic late-cycle moves. Forward bit growth in 2027 will be 25–30%+ vs 16% in 2026, and HBM gluts have happened before (HBM2e/HBM3 had brief gluts in 2019 and 2022).

**Working call**: This is a 2018-equivalent cyclical peak with HBM-specific structural support. We are likely in the 8th–9th inning of *general* DRAM tightness, but only the 5th–6th inning of HBM tightness given the longer lead times for hybrid-bonding capacity.

## 6. Bull vs Bear Debate

### Bull Case
1. **Physical bottlenecks compound through the stack**: HBM requires CoWoS at TSMC (2-yr lead time), TSV equipment at AMAT/Lam, hybrid bonding at BESI, and 1c/HBM4 logic. No single ramp solves the stack — this isn't a 6-month inventory cycle.
2. **Demand step-change, not blip**: Hyperscaler capex run-rates are at $400B+ and rising; Samsung 1Q26 chip OP was ~50x YoY. This isn't smartphone DRAM — it's structural compute infrastructure.
3. **Inventory is at all-time lows** (2–3 weeks Hynix, 6 weeks Samsung) and CSPs are signing **multi-year LTAs** for 2027 supply — the demand signal is being expressed in contract structure, not just spot.
4. **Industry is more disciplined**: Three-player oligopoly with rational behavior; commodity DDR4 even has supply constraint by extending lifecycles vs flooding the market.

### Bear Case
1. **Capacity expansions are real and large**: Samsung +50% HBM (170k→250k wafers/mo), SK Hynix DRAM doubling 2H26 to 600k wafers/mo, Micron +23% capex. These are the exact capacity decisions made at every prior cycle peak.
2. **Margins at 70%+ are unsustainable** — they invariably revert; SK Hynix 1Q26 OP margin of 72% is not a steady state, it is a peak.
3. **HBM gluts have happened before**: HBM2e in 2019, HBM3 briefly in 2022. As HBM4 ramps in 2026/2027 and HBM4e in 2027/2028 with all three players competing aggressively, supply could outrun even AI demand.
4. **Multiple compression risk**: At 6.8x P/B Hynix has no historical precedent. Even modest forward EBITDA cuts compounded with multiple compression imply 30–50% downside scenarios.

### Key Catalysts to Resolve the Debate
1. **Nvidia results (May 28 / Aug 2026)**: Hyperscaler capex confirmation/cut.
2. **Micron F4Q26 earnings (late Sep 2026)**: Forward DRAM/HBM commentary, especially HBM4 ramp commentary.
3. **Hynix 2H26 capacity reveal**: Whether the stated 600k DRAM wafer/month plan slips or accelerates.
4. **HBM4 sampling (mid–late 2026)**: Yield rates from Samsung (catching up), Hynix (incumbent), Micron (gaining share).
5. **Spot/contract spread sustainability**: A spot price rollover for >2 months while contracts hold would be the canonical late-cycle signal.
