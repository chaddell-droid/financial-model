## Executive Summary

The 1Q26 earnings cycle (reported April 22 – April 30, 2026) revealed an AI memory supercycle so severe that all three majors are essentially supply-constrained on every advanced product. The picture, however, is far from symmetric:

- **SK Hynix** remains the AI-memory bellwether with a record **72% operating margin** in 1Q26 ([TrendForce, Apr 23, 2026](https://www.trendforce.com/news/2026/04/23/news-sk-hynix-reports-5x-1q26-profit-surge-operating-margin-hits-72-outpacing-tsmc-and-micron/)) and ~**two-thirds allocation** of Nvidia's HBM4 demand for Vera Rubin ([TrendForce, Jan 28, 2026](https://www.trendforce.com/news/2026/01/28/news-sk-hynix-reportedly-to-supply-about-two-thirds-of-nvidia-hbm4-samsung-targets-early-delivery/)).
- **Samsung Electronics** has staged a credible comeback after a brutal 18-month HBM3E delay, finally clearing 12-high HBM3E qualification with Nvidia in September 2025 and beginning HBM4 mass-production sales for Nvidia's Vera Rubin platform in 1Q26 ([Samsung Newsroom, Apr 30, 2026](https://news.samsung.com/global/samsung-electronics-announces-first-quarter-2026-results)). DS Division operating margin hit **65.7%** ([TrendForce, Apr 30, 2026](https://www.trendforce.com/news/2026/04/30/news-samsungs-ds-division-tops-nvidia-with-65-7-operating-margin-driving-93-8-of-1q26-profit-on-memory-boom/)).
- **Micron** is the cleanest pure-play. FY2Q26 (ended Feb 26, 2026) delivered **$23.86B revenue** (+196% YoY) at a **75% gross margin** with **$12.07 GAAP EPS** ([Micron IR, Mar 18, 2026](https://investors.micron.com/news-releases/news-release-details/micron-technology-inc-reports-results-second-quarter-fiscal-2026)). Calendar 2026 HBM supply is sold out; FY3Q26 guidance calls for **$33.5B revenue at ~81% gross margin**.

All three stocks have re-rated dramatically — SK Hynix is up ~768% on a 1-year basis, Samsung is up ~387%, and Micron is up >125% YTD 2026 — but on forward earnings, **SK Hynix at ~5.9x forward P/E and Micron at ~8.1x forward P/E** still screen cheap on a peak-earnings basis. The bull case for each is HBM mix; the bear case is symmetric — that 2026 is the cycle peak.

### Strategic Position: Catching Up or Losing Ground?

**Catching up — but from behind, not from parity.** The 18-month HBM3E delay cost Samsung the Blackwell cycle entirely; the recovery story rests on HBM4 timing. Two things are true at once:

1. Samsung is the *only* fully-integrated player (memory + foundry + advanced packaging — "zHBM" turnkey thesis).
2. SK Hynix still has the architecture and customer trust lead on HBM4. Samsung's >30% HBM4 share with Nvidia is real but secondary to SK Hynix's two-thirds.

The conventional-DRAM tailwind currently masks weaker relative HBM economics. If 2026's conventional DRAM ASP move reverses, Samsung's mix-disadvantage vs. SK Hynix re-emerges.

## Cross-Cutting Analysis

### 1. Who is winning HBM share, and why?

**Tier order today (Q3 2025 last clean datapoint, with 1Q26 directional commentary):**
1. **SK Hynix ~57-59%** — incumbency, Nvidia trust, two-thirds of HBM4 Vera Rubin allocation
2. **Samsung ~22%** (and rising toward 30%+) — recovered after 18-month delay; HBM4 first-mass-production claim narrows the gap
3. **Micron ~21%** — third by share but cleanest customer story (Nvidia + AMD), fully sold out

**Why the trajectory is shifting:**
- SK Hynix's lead is structurally narrowing because HBM4 is a more diversified competitive arena (multiple architectures, multiple customer SKUs).
- Samsung's HBM4 first-mass-production with Nvidia Vera Rubin is the most important positive datapoint of the cycle for them.
- Micron's strategic clarity (Crucial exit, 3-5 year sold-out contracts, AMD wins) gives them the best framework on a per-dollar-of-revenue basis.

### 2. Best mix shift toward server/HBM

- **Micron** has the cleanest mix shift — exited consumer entirely, ~56% of revenue in CMBU+CDBU, and moving higher.
- **SK Hynix** is also heavily server/HBM tilted but still carries mobile DRAM and consumer NAND.
- **Samsung Memory** has the largest absolute HBM revenue but the broadest tail (mobile DRAM, consumer DDR5, foundry coupling), making it the *least* pure-play.

### 3. Margin trajectory

| Co. | 1Q26 OP Margin | Direction |
|---|---|---|
| **SK Hynix (group)** | **72%** | At/near peak; HBM mix supportive but capex stepping up |
| **Samsung DS** | **65.7%** | Still rising — HBM4 ramp + DRAM ASP tailwind |
| **Micron (group)** | ~63% (GAAP OP margin implied; 75% gross, opex leverage) | **Rising** to ~75% gross at FY3Q26 guide; cleanest expansion |

All three are at or near record margins. SK Hynix and Samsung look closest to peak; Micron's guide implies further sequential margin expansion in FY3Q26.

### 4. Cleanest pure-play on the AI memory thesis

**Micron, by a clear margin:**
- 100% memory exposure (no foundry, no mobile, no displays)
- Crucial consumer exit removes the last commodity drag
- HBM + high-cap DIMM + LP server DRAM at $10B in FY25, ramping
- Fully sold out 2026; 3-5 year price/volume agreements
- Customer footprint: Nvidia + AMD, with explicit HBM4 design wins

SK Hynix is a strong second pure-play but carries NAND (~21% of mix) and Korean conglomerate considerations.

Samsung is *not* a pure-play — DS is ~91% memory but the equity reflects the entire group (mobile, displays, foundry, consumer electronics). For investors who want the AI memory trade, Samsung dilutes the thesis.
